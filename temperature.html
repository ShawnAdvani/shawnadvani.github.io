Temperature Levels




This plot analyses the difference in temperature levels across (degrees Celsius) the different humidity lines. Looking at the plot, there seems to be a clear and significant correlation between latitude and temperatures. The points at the lowest and highest latitude levels have the lowest temperature levels (10 to -10 degrees) and the latitude range 0 to 10 show to have the highest temperature levels (15 to almost 40 degrees discounting outliers). This makes it safe to assume that temperatures are generally higher closer to the equator and generally lower closer to the poles. One thing to take into account is the tilt of the earth in proportion to rotation and how the sun impacts distribution of direct sunlight across the earth. This would explain why the highest recorded temperature is north of the equator; the data was collected during northern hemisphere summer.
